Prof. Ogunduyile

Professor Sunday Roberts Ogunduyile is a Professor of Industrial Design (Textiles). Prof. Ogunduyile was an Ag. Head of Department, Arts and Material Development of the Institute of Education, A.B.U., former Head of Department of Industrial Design, Federal University of Technology, Akure. He rose through the ranks to become a Professor in 2002. He served in many Management positions such as: Dean, School of Environmental Technology, FUTA, Member of Governing Council, FUTA and Ondo State University of Science and Technology (OSUSTECH), Director, FUTA Business Development Centre, and Vice-Chancellor, Olusegun Agagu University of Science and Technology, Okitipupa. Professor Ogunduyile has contributed immensely to the advancement of knowledge in Industrial Design and Textiles. He has published several articles in reputable national and international journals. He has served as external examiners to many Universities in Nigeria and abroad where he supervised several Post Graduate Theses. Prof. Ogunduyile belongs to many Professional Associations.

Dr. Mrs. Bridget Awosika

Dr. Mrs. Bridget Awosika is a well known motivational speaker in Ondo kingdom. Her passion for youth development is second to none. Chief lecturer at Adeyemi Federal University of Education, Ondo. She studied Home economics from the prestigious Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile Ife and also holds a Phd. in Textile and Clothing. She’s a member, Internationational federal for Home economics (IFHE), member, Housing Education Research Association (HERA) and Fellow, National Institute of Administration.

Awosika Mitchele Oluwatosin

Mitchele Oluwastosin is a senior Data analyst at Amazon Technologies (USA). She holds a bachelor's degree in Agricultural Technology from the Federal University of Technology, Akure and a Master's degree in Technology Management from North Carolina Agricultural and Technical State University, USA. Oluwastosin is an experienced Data analyst with various certifications, including that of Harvard business analytics program.

Omolayo Catherine Akinrimisi

Omolayo Catherine Akinrimisi is a Nigerian Registered Nurse Midwife and one time Nursing Sister at the General hospital Ondo. She obtained a B.Sc. in Nursing and Master in public Health at Grand Crayon University in Arizona, USA. She has worked in many State Hospitals - Grady Hospital , kindred Hospital and Emory University Teaching Hospital in Atlanta Georgia. She is the CEO of KandK Healthcare Systems Inc., a private Home Care that provides Personal Support Care and Skilled Nursing for the elderly, mentally challenged individuals and children in the US of since 2006. She is also a Medicaid Provider at Stockbridge and Warner Robins in Georgia, USA. She is a members of the Linkway Diplomats Inc. that provides for communities with critical needs. Catherine is a realtor both in Nigeria and the USA.

ADE-AYOOLA Fadejogun Oluwamayokun

Fadejogun Oluwamayokun is a registered Nurse having attended the Hackney College for Nursing in London. She studied Business Administration at the Metropolitan University, Holloway after which she obtained a Higher Diploma Certificate in Media Training from the Events and Community Portsmouth University in London and the Scholars International Institute of Technology in the United States. She has held numerous positions with the Respiratory lntensive Care Specialist in Ramsay Healthcare (NHS) London, UK; Regional Manager, Citigroup Financial Services Canary Wharf, London, UK; She is the CEO of Bloomingdales Store and Hair Command, East London; CEO- Presenter of OECS Media Online, Ondo Eki Chat Show Worldwide, the Global Yoruba Media Online Concerts and Events London; Fadejogun is a former Cabin Crew with the Nigeria Airways Ltd, Lagos, Nigeria.

Prof. Olukoya Ogen

Prof. Olukoya Ogen is the Director of Research and Innovation Management at Osun State University, Osogbo. He is also an Endowed Professor of Education at ICT University in Yaoundé, a Visiting Senior Research Fellow at the Department of African Studies and Anthropology at the University of Birmingham, and a 2022 LUCAS-LAHRI Visiting Fellow at the University of Leeds. He presently serves on the Tetfund National Research Fund Committee and has received multiple international research and travel awards in addition to a variety of postdoctoral fellowships from the United States and the United Kingdom. From 2012 to 2017, he served as the country director for a five-year research program financed by the European Research Council on "Everyday Religious Encounter in Southwest Yorubaland." In 2019 he received the Ali Mazrui Prize for Excellence in Scholarship, and in 2021 the Chief Data Officer and Information Quality (MIT CDOIQ) Program presented him with the Chief Data Officer Ambassador Award. He is a fellow of the Historical Society of Nigeria, a fellow of the Chartered Institute of Educational Management and Administration, a fellow of the Institute of Information Management, and the recently-retired provost of the Adeyemi College of Education in Ondo. He is also a member of the Nigerian Academy of Letters.
